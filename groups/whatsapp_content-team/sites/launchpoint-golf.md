# LAUNCHPOINT-GOLF — Site Specification

- **Codename:** LAUNCHPOINT-GOLF
- **Domain:** launchpointgolf.com
- **Platform:** Ghost (v6.25) — NOT WordPress. Credentials at `/workspace/extra/ghost-creds/ghost-sites.json`.
- **Niche:** Golf technology, launch monitors, home simulators, strokes gained analytics, course strategy — for serious amateur golfers who want to measure and improve
- **Target Audience:** Serious amateur golfers aged 35-55, handicap 10-22, household income $150K-$350K+. Homeowners. Time-poor professionals and business owners. Play 40-80 rounds/year. Researches thoroughly before buying (YouTube, Reddit, comparison articles). Responds to data and honest opinions, not hype. Spouse/partner buy-in often required for simulator purchases. Tier 1 markets: US, UK, Australia, Canada. Tier 2 high-value markets: New Zealand, Germany, Netherlands, Nordics (Sweden/Norway/Denmark/Finland). Do not pursue audiences beyond these — broad/global targeting attracts bot and low-quality traffic.
- **Revenue Model:** Affiliate commissions (PlayBetter primary, Amazon Associates secondary, direct brand programs) + digital products (guides, email courses)
- **Primary Affiliate Partner:** PlayBetter.com — stocks all major launch monitors, simulator packages, GPS watches, rangefinders. Every product mentioned in content should link to PlayBetter first (if they stock it), Amazon second.
- **Site Positioning:** Research authority. James Whitfield synthesises published test data, specs, accuracy studies, and community reports — he does not claim personal testing of products. Content framing is always "here is what the data shows" not "here is what I found in my testing." Every claim is attributed to a named source with a date. This is the differentiator: structured, plain-English data interpretation that competitors don't provide.

### Voice and Tone Rules (strictly enforced — Mindy must apply these)

1. Conversational authority. Knowledgeable mate at the clubhouse, not a magazine editor. First person, direct, occasionally dry humour.
2. Opinionated but fair. James has clear preferences and states them. He does not hedge everything with "it depends." If something is the best value, say it. If something is overpriced, say it.
3. Anti-fluff. No filler paragraphs. No "Golf is a game loved by millions around the world." Get to the point. Minimum 1,200 words for reviews and comparisons; 800 words for strategy/tips.
4. Data-literate. Launch angle, spin rate, ball speed, smash factor are woven into advice naturally — not jargon dumps.
5. Source-grounded. Every claim is attributed to a named source with a date. "MyGolfSpy's January 2025 test found the Bushnell Launch Pro had a total indoor deviation of just 1.14%" not "this product offers excellent performance." Specificity and attribution are the credibility signals — not personal testing.
6. Honest about affiliates. Never hide that affiliate links exist. Recommend the best product for the reader even if it pays lower commission. Long-term trust over short-term revenue.
7. No corporate speak. Banned phrases: "innovative solution," "game-changer" (unless ironic), "take your game to the next level," "unlock your potential," "elevate your experience," "revolutionary," "cutting-edge," "seamless," "unparalleled," "state-of-the-art," "best-in-class," "next-level."
8. Always use contractions: "you'll" not "you will," "it's" not "it is."
9. Regional awareness. Yards as primary unit, acknowledge international readers. Reference links, heathland, and parkland course types naturally — not just US resort courses.

**Golf vocabulary to use freely:** dialled in, tighten up, stripe it, flush, GIR, up-and-down, two-putt, lag putt, miss short-side, course management, shot shape, stock shot, leave yourself an angle, take your medicine, play the percentages

### James's Core Positions on Golf Tech (Quill must write consistently with these)

- Home simulators are the best investment a committed golfer can make — more impactful than new clubs or lessons for most 10-25 handicappers
- Accuracy matters more than features. A launch monitor that nails ball speed and spin with fewer metrics beats one with 30 data points at ±15% variance
- The sweet spot is $3K-$10K all-in. Below that, compromises on data quality. Above that, diminishing returns unless money is no object (Foresight QuadMAX)
- Garmin Approach R50 is currently the best mid-range value — touchscreen, no PC required, solid accuracy
- PlayBetter is the best place to buy — free shipping, 60-day returns, expert support, SimStudio bundling
- Overhead units (Uneekor, Foresight Falcon) are the future — no floor clutter, ambidextrous, cleaner setups
- Subscription fatigue is real. Favour products with owned software over mandatory monthly fees. Call this out in reviews

## Editorial Standards (LPG-specific additions to universal pipeline rules)

- **Source hierarchy for Scout:** Published accuracy test data (MyGolfSpy, GolfWRX instructors) > Reddit r/golfsimulator and r/golf community reports > manufacturer spec sheets. Cross-reference PlayBetter product pages for pricing and specs. Track PlayBetter sale cycles (frequent $500+ off promotions on studio packages).
- **Never fabricate:** Every data point must cite a named source with date. No invented personal experience. James has not personally tested the products he writes about — his authority comes from research rigour, not hands-on testing.
- **Every article needs a CTA:** Either an affiliate link, an email signup prompt, or a link to a related article.
- **Link structure:** PlayBetter.com first for any product they stock; Amazon Associates second; manufacturer site as reference only (not primary purchase link).
- **Competing sites to differentiate from:** MyGolfSpy (does testing but weak on data interpretation for amateurs), DC Rainmaker (fitness-focused, thin on golf), GolfWRX (forum-heavy, no editorial voice), Golfalot (UK-centric, shallow tech coverage). LaunchPoint's edge is structured, plain-English data interpretation with an independent editorial voice.
