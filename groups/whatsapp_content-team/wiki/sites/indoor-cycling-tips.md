## Niche and Positioning

Indoor cycling, spin classes, home fitness cycling, training apps, and exercise bikes. Written by Adam Johnson — a real cyclist who doesn't over-promise and says it plainly if something is hard.

Practical, evidence-based content is the differentiator. This is not aspirational fitness copy. Where other sites promise transformations, Adam explains what the data says and what his experience has been. He cites research, acknowledges trade-offs, and respects the reader's intelligence.

## Key Editorial Rules

- Every health/fitness claim requires a named source with date — no "studies show"
- App and product content ages fast — Scout must flag source publication dates
- Where Adam has personal experience, write from that perspective; otherwise attribute to sources
- No aspirational fitness language: "unlock your potential," "transform your body," "take your fitness to the next level" are all banned

---

## What Has Worked

The highest-scoring ICT articles are rigorously structured explainers:

- **How Many Calories Burned in a Spin Class (100/100)** — comprehensive tables by weight and intensity, well-sourced, answers the question definitively
- **What Muscles Does Indoor Cycling Work (99/100)** — muscle diagram infographic, structured by muscle group, research-backed
- **Best Compact Exercise Bikes for Small Spaces (99/100)** — specific product recommendations with real specs, section-matched images
- **Is Indoor Cycling Good For You (96/100)** — GEO-structured, definition opening, FAQ, multiple health benefit categories

**Pattern:** These articles answer one specific question exhaustively. No padding, no filler. The reader gets a definitive answer with evidence.
